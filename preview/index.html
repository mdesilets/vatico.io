02 · what aesthetics is decided on today

Quarterly surveys.

Agency decks.

Field-rep anecdote.

A multi-billion-dollar consumer category, decided on the inputs of a private-equity diligence binder from two quarters ago. Brands manage their portfolios on field intuition. Manufacturers manage launches on attribution that arrives a year late. Operators acquire chains on the same anecdote everyone else has access to. The category outgrew its tools years ago and has been operating without replacements.

The Aesthetics Index is the alternative.

03 · what the Index holds

Six things, held continuously.

i.

Practice record.

Every operational practice in the category, identified durably and held through rebrand, ownership change, and chain consolidation. Multi-location chains are held as chains, not as duplicates.

Diligence on a chain takes hours, not weeks.

ii.

Industry ontology.

Every manufacturer, brand, brand family, product, generic equivalent, category, and subcategory currently in market — held as a formal model under change control.

Every query is asked in the language the category already speaks.

iii.

Channel observation.

Continuous monitoring of the public-facing channels practices broadcast on. Per-channel cadence. No gaps.

Activity is read the week it happens, not the quarter it surfaces.

iv.

Event extraction.

Each clinical and commercial decision a practice makes — adoption, displacement, service addition, device acquisition, rebrand, closure — captured as a timestamped event attached to the practice's record.

Trajectories, not snapshots. A record, not a directory.

v.

Brand-level rollups.

Practice-mention share, displacement velocity, adoption curves, whitespace — sliced by metro, manufacturer, vertical, or arbitrary cohort. Trailing windows configurable.

Share is read the way ad spend is read: continuously, comparably, at the resolution of the decision.

vi.

Supply against demand.

Practice-side observation paired with consumer-side observation at metro resolution. Where the category is asked for, read against where it is offered.

Vacancies and saturations are visible before they're announced.

04 · Monday morning, with the Index

What the Index lets you do.

  1. Spot the brand cresting in a metro before your competitor does.
  2. Quantify the practices that switched away from your menu last quarter, with the brand they switched to and the week they made the move.
  3. Find the metros where category demand outruns practice supply — and the practices best positioned to absorb it.
  4. Read what the consolidator did to the chain after they bought it: every menu change, every new device, every rebrand.
  5. Identify the practices that look like your best customers and that you've never sold into.
  6. Track the displacement front in energy devices across the trailing six quarters.

06 · the posture

One Index.

07 · the inflection

The category outgrew its tools.

For a decade, every clinical and commercial decision in this category has been broadcast in public. The raw material has existed.

The tolerance for operating without one has ended.
